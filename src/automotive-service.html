What to Do If You Lock Your Keys Inside Your Car

It happens to everyone – you shut the door, hear the lock click, and realize your keys are still inside. First, don’t panic. Check all doors and the trunk; sometimes one may remain unlocked. If you have a spare key nearby, try retrieving it before taking further steps.

If that’s not an option, the safest solution is to call a licensed automotive locksmith. Modern vehicles have complex locking systems, and attempting to open the door yourself can damage the car. A professional can unlock it quickly and safely, often without leaving a scratch.

How to Avoid Damage When Unlocking a Car

DIY methods, like using coat hangers or screwdrivers, can easily damage door seals, scratch paint, or break internal mechanisms. Licensed locksmiths use specialized tools designed to unlock cars without causing harm. They’re trained to handle different makes and models, including high-security and keyless systems.

Choosing a professional not only protects your vehicle but also saves you money in the long run. Many locksmiths offer 24/7 emergency services, so you can get help whenever you need it – safely and efficiently.
